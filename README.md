Namma Neeru – Bengaluru’s Vanishing Lakes & Civic Action Portal Water is one of the most essential natural resources for all forms of life. It supports agriculture, industry, biodiversity, and daily human needs. However, in recent decades, the availability and quality of water have come under severe threat due to rapid urbanization, pollution, encroachment, and inefficient management practices. The global water crisis is not just about scarcity — it’s about the imbalance between demand and responsible usage. Bengaluru, once celebrated as the “City of Lakes”, had over 260 interconnected lakes that helped maintain its unique ecosystem. These lakes not only served as sources of drinking water and groundwater recharge but also supported agriculture, fishing, and biodiversity. Unfortunately, unchecked urban sprawl, improper waste disposal, and encroachment have led to the disappearance or degradation of most of these water bodies. Today, only a handful remain functional, while others are polluted or converted into residential and commercial areas.   
To counter this alarming situation, the “Namma Neeru” civic portal was developed as a communitydriven digital initiative aimed at reviving Bengaluru’s lost water heritage. The platform acts as an interactive awareness and engagement hub that educates citizens about water conservation, lake ecology, and sustainable practices. It empowers people to take part in local clean-up drives, report encroachments, monitor water quality, and share success stories of restoration efforts.   
The portal also connects users with environmental NGOs, civic bodies, and research organizations working on lake rejuvenation projects. Through real-time data, informative articles, interactive maps, and volunteer registration options, Namma Neeru bridges the gap between awareness and action. It inspires citizens to take ownership of their surroundings and contribute to the city’s ecological balance. Carrying the message “Every Drop Counts – Join the Movement”, Namma Neeru emphasizes that sustainable water management is not just a government responsibility but a collective civic duty. Every small action — from reducing household water waste to participating in lake clean-ups — contributes to a larger goal of restoring Bengaluru’s water legacy.  
  
In essence, Namma Neeru is more than just a portal; it is a citizen-led movement striving to bring back the city’s lakes, protect its water resources, and build a greener, more sustainable future for generations to come. It inspires individuals to become guardians of their environment, transforming awareness into collective action. By uniting communities, civic bodies, and environmental groups on a single platform, Namma Neeru strengthens Bengaluru’s fight against water pollution and lake degradation. It nurtures a sense of responsibility among citizens, reminding them that every small effort contributes to a larger environmental impact. The initiative also serves as a living testament to the power of digital technology in driving social change and environmental activism. Ultimately, Namma Neeru aims to revive Bengaluru’s disappearing water bodies, restore lost ecosystems, and ensure that future generations inherit a city rich in natural beauty and ecological harmony.
